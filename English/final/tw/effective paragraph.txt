Unity: focuses on a single main idea or topic, and all sentences in the paragraph support this idea.

Coherence: sentences are logically and smoothly connected and the order of sentences makes sense.

Clarity: the meaning is easily understood and the language used is simple and concise.

Development: provides enough detail and evidence to fully explain the main idea.

Focus: begins with a clear topic sentence that states the main idea and keeps the paragraph on track.

Organization: sentences are arranged in a logical order and follow a clear structure.

Transitions: uses transitional words and phrases to connect sentences and help the paragraph flow smoothly.

Proper length: not too long or too short, a good paragraph is usually 4-7 sentences in length.
