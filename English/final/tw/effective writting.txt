Clear topic sentence: states the main idea of the paragraph and sets the tone for the rest of the content.

Supporting sentences: provide evidence and additional information to support the main idea.

Coherence: sentences are logically connected and follow a clear and concise structure.

Unity: all sentences in the paragraph focus on a single main idea.

Development: provides enough detail and evidence to fully explain the main idea.

Clarity: the meaning is easily understood and the language used is simple and concise.

Conciseness: avoids unnecessary words or details that distract from the main idea.

Transitions: uses transitional words and phrases to connect sentences and help the paragraph flow smoothly.

By following these guidelines, you can write clear, concise, 
and well-structured paragraphs that effectively convey your ideas to your audience.
